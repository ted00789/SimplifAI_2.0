## üìä The Real Cost of Hiring a Receptionist in the U.S.

![Human vs AI receptionist cost comparison](https://www.simplifai-solutions.com/images/blogs/ai-receptionists-worth-it-2026-real-cost-breakdown/human_vs_AI_cost.jpeg)

If you are considering hiring someone to answer your phones, you need to look at more than hourly pay.

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics publishes wage data for receptionists and information clerks, including median hourly wages and annual earnings. You can review the official numbers here:

[U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics ‚Äì Receptionists Wage Data](https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes434171.htm)

According to BLS data, the median hourly wage for receptionists is in the high teens per hour. At 40 hours per week, that quickly turns into a base annual salary in the mid to high $30,000 range.

But that is not your true cost.

You must also account for:

- Employer payroll taxes  
- Federal and state unemployment insurance  
- Workers compensation  
- Paid time off  
- Training time  
- Equipment and workspace  

The Small Business Administration explains employer tax responsibilities here:

[SBA ‚Äì Employer Payroll and Tax Responsibilities](https://www.sba.gov/business-guide/manage-your-business/pay-taxes)

When you combine wages and employment overhead, many small businesses realistically spend between $45,000 and $55,000 per year for a reliable full-time receptionist.

And that still only covers business hours.

![Closed business but customers are calling](https://www.simplifai-solutions.com/images/blogs/ai-receptionists-worth-it-2026-real-cost-breakdown/closed_but_people_call.jpeg)

Here is where the conversation changes.

Most business owners assume the majority of calls happen between 9 AM and 5 PM. That used to be true. It is not anymore.

A recent small business communication study found that approximately 67 percent of customer calls occur outside traditional business hours.

You can see reporting on that data here:

[Small Business Communication Study ‚Äì After Hours Call Data](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/callvera-ai_recent-research-reveals-a-significant-gap-activity-7402002530026729472-wU4R)

Additional industry reporting shows that more than half of inbound calls now happen after 5 PM, especially in service industries where customers research during the day and call at night.

[Unicom Corp ‚Äì Overnight Answering Service Insights](https://www.unicomcorp.com/blog/overnight-answering-service/)

Think about that for a moment.

If most calls happen when you are closed, and you only staff phones during business hours, you are not comparing a $50,000 employee to a $5,000 AI system.

You are comparing coverage to no coverage.

And that is a revenue problem.
